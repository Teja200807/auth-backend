Explore Munnar

Queen of Hill Stations

Munnar, meaning "three rivers" in Malayalam, is a picturesque hill station nestled in the Western Ghats of Kerala. Once the summer resort of the British administration, Munnar today is famous for its sprawling tea plantations, misty valleys, and cool climate.

ðŸŒ¿ Tea Capital of South India

Munnar's economy revolves around tea plantations established by British planters in the late 19th century. The region produces some of the world's finest Orthodox tea, with the Tata Tea Museum preserving this rich heritage.

The region's biodiversity is exceptional, with Eravikulam National Park serving as the last refuge of the endangered Nilgiri Tahr. Munnar's unique ecosystem includes Shola forests and grasslands that support diverse flora and fauna.

Popular as both a honeymoon destination and family getaway, Munnar offers activities ranging from plantation tours and trekking to wildlife spotting and spice garden visits. The Neelakurinji flower that blooms once every 12 years paints the hills blue, attracting nature enthusiasts worldwide.

ðŸŒ„ British Colonial Legacy

Munnar's development began in 1877 when the British discovered its potential for tea cultivation. Many colonial-era bungalows and churches still stand, adding to the region's old-world charm.

Top 10 Natural Attractions

Tea Gardens

Endless rolling hills covered in lush green tea plantations.

Eravikulam National Park

UNESCO site home to endangered Nilgiri Tahr mountain goats.

Mattupetty Dam

Scenic reservoir offering boating and stunning mountain views.

Anamudi Peak

South India's highest peak at 2,695 meters.

Top Station

Viewpoint offering panoramic views of Western Ghats valleys.

Echo Point

Popular spot where voices echo across the Kundala Lake.

Tata Tea Museum

Learn about tea processing and colonial plantation history.

Attukal Waterfalls

Picturesque cascading waterfalls surrounded by forests.

Christ Church

Historic 1910 Anglican church with beautiful stained glass.

Blossom Hydel Park

Serene park with exotic flowers, boating, and picnic spots.
