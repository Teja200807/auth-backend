About Lambasingi

Lambasingi, also known as Lammasingi, is a small village in the Eastern Ghats famous for being the only place in South India that experiences snowfall and sub-zero temperatures. Located at 1,000 meters (3,300 ft) above sea level, it's called the "Kashmir of Andhra Pradesh."

Lambasingi experiences temperatures as low as 0¬∞C in winter (Dec-Feb), with occasional frost and mist. The area recorded snowfall in 1998, 2006, and 2018, attracting tourists seeking cold weather in South India.

The village is surrounded by coffee, pepper, and apple plantations, with lush green valleys and mist-covered hills. The local tribal community, mostly belonging to the Bagata tribe, practices traditional farming and weaving.

Lambasingi is part of the Chintapalli Mandal and is approximately 100 km from Visakhapatnam. The journey involves winding roads through beautiful forests and offers panoramic views of the Eastern Ghats.

The region is rich in biodiversity with numerous medicinal plants, orchids, and wildlife including deer, peacocks, and various bird species. It's part of the Kambalakonda Wildlife Sanctuary corridor.
