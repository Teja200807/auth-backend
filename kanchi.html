City of Thousand Temples

Kanchipuram, known as the "City of Thousand Temples," is one of India's seven sacred cities and a major pilgrimage center. Flourishing as the capital of the Pallava dynasty between the 6th and 8th centuries, Kanchi showcases the evolution of Dravidian temple architecture across multiple dynasties.

Kanchi features unique Dravidian architecture spanning Pallava, Chola, and Vijayanagara periods. The Ekambareswarar Temple covers 25 acres with a 1000-pillared hall, while the Kailasanathar Temple is the city's oldest surviving structure from the 8th century.

The city is home to four Divya Desams (among 108 Vishnu temples) and three of the five Pancha Bhoota Stalams (Shiva temples representing elements). Kanchi has been a major center for Hindu philosophy, where Adi Shankara established the Advaita Vedanta school at the Kamakshi Amman Temple.

Beyond spirituality, Kanchi is world-renowned for its silk sarees, woven by master weavers for generations. These sarees are considered among India's finest, featuring traditional motifs and zari work that take weeks to complete.

üßµ Silk Capital of India

Kanchipuram silk sarees are woven with pure mulberry silk and zari (gold thread), featuring traditional motifs like peacocks, temples, and floral patterns. Each saree takes 15-20 days to weave and can last for decades.

Top 10 Temple Attractions

Ekambareswarar Temple

Pancha Bhoota Stalam (Earth), 25 acres, 1000-pillared hall, ancient mango tree.

Kailasanathar Temple

8th century Pallava architecture, pure sandstone, earliest structural temple.

Varadharaja Perumal Temple

Divya Desam, 32-acre complex with 100-pillared hall and exquisite sculptures.

Kamakshi Amman Temple

Important Shakti Peetha, Adi Shankara established Sri Chakra here.

Kumarakottam Temple

Dedicated to Murugan, famous for grand Skanda Sashti festival celebrations.

Ulagalandar Perumal Temple

Divya Desam featuring 35-feet tall standing image of Vishnu (Trivikrama).

Silk Weaving Centers

Traditional Kanchipuram silk saree weaving centers and showrooms.

Kanchi Math

Monastery established by Adi Shankara, center of Advaita Vedanta philosophy.

Vaikunta Perumal Temple

8th century Pallava temple with unique three-tier shrine structure.

Handicraft Villages

Traditional craft villages producing bronze idols, stone carvings, and jewelry.
