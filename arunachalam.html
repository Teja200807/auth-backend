Sacred History & Significance

Arunachaleswarar Temple, one of Pancha Bhoota Sthalams representing fire element, was constructed by Chola kings between 850-1280 AD covering 25 acres. Legend states Lord Shiva manifested as infinite fire column during dispute with Vishnu, hence Agni Lingam. The temple's massive gopurams (4th tallest at 217 ft) were built by Vijayanagara kings in 16th century.

This sacred site represents the Fire element among the five Pancha Bhoota Stalams. The main deity is worshipped as Arunachaleswarar (Shiva) and Unnamalai Amman (Parvati).

Saint Ramana Maharshi attained enlightenment at this hill in 1896, establishing global spiritual significance. Annual Karthigai Deepam festival sees 3-ft oil lamp lit atop Arunachala Hill visible from 30km, drawing 3 million devotees. The temple follows unique rituals including Girivalam (14km circumambulation) on full moon days.

Unmarried Parvati (Aparna) worshipped here, making it ideal for marriage-related prayers. The 1000-pillared hall and thousand-linga hall are architectural marvels. British records from 19th century mention temple's vast land holdings and economic influence.

The 14km circumambulation around Arunachala Hill on full moon nights is believed to cleanse sins and bestow spiritual blessings. The path contains 8 lingams and numerous sacred spots.
