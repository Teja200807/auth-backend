Temple History & Significance

The Kashi Vishwanath Temple, located on the western bank of the holy Ganges in Varanasi, is one of the most revered Hindu temples dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, considered the holiest of Shiva temples. The temple has been destroyed and rebuilt several times throughout history.

1194 AD: Original temple destroyed by Qutb-ud-din Aibak
1585 AD: Rebuilt by Gujarati merchant under Akbar's reign
1669 AD: Destroyed again by Aurangzeb, who built Gyanvapi Mosque
1780 AD: Current structure built by Ahilyabai Holkar of Indore
1839 AD: Gold plating donated by Maharaja Ranjit Singh

The temple complex consists of a series of smaller shrines located in a small lane called Vishwanath Gali, near Dasaswamedh Ghat. The main deity is Shiva in the form of a lingam, 60 cm tall and 90 cm in circumference, housed in a silver altar.

Hindus believe that a visit to Kashi Vishwanath Temple leads to moksha (liberation from the cycle of birth and death). The temple is mentioned in the Skanda Purana and is considered the first Jyotirlinga. Devotees offer water from the Ganges to the lingam and perform the Rudrabhishek puja.

Architecture & Features

Golden Spire

The temple's three domes are covered with 800 kg of pure gold plating donated by Maharaja Ranjit Singh in 1839.

Jnana Vapi

The "Well of Wisdom" located within the complex, believed to contain the original Shiva lingam for protection.

88 Steps

Steep stairs connecting the temple to the Dashashwamedh Ghat on the banks of the Ganges river.

Temple Layout

East-facing entrance leads to the main sanctum through a silver-plated doorway with intricate carvings.

Silver Sanctum

The inner sanctum walls are covered with silver plates depicting various Hindu deities and scenes.

Ganges Proximity

Located just 300 meters from the holy Ganges river, allowing for ritual bathing before worship.

Spiritual Significance

Jyotirlinga

One of the 12 Jyotirlingas (pillars of light) where Shiva appeared as a fiery column of light.

Moksha Dham

Believed that death in Kashi ensures liberation from the cycle of rebirth (moksha).

First Jyotirlinga

Considered the first and most important Jyotirlinga according to scriptures.

How to Reach

  • Air: Lal Bahadur Shastri Airport (25km)
  • Train: Varanasi Junction (6km)
  • Road: Well connected from all cities
  • Local: Auto, Taxi, Cycle Rickshaw

Important Tips

  • Mobile phones not allowed inside
  • Dress modestly, traditional wear preferred
  • Arrive early to avoid long queues
  • Carry minimum belongings
  • Book online darshan in peak season
  • Respect temple customs and traditions
