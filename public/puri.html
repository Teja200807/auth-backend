Detailed History

The Jagannath Temple in Puri is an important Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Jagannath, a form of Vishnu. Located on the eastern coast of India, the temple was built in the 11th century by King Anantavarman Chodaganga Deva of the Eastern Ganga dynasty.

The temple is one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites and features Kalinga architecture. The main temple complex covers an area of 400,000 square feet and is surrounded by a 20-foot high wall.

The temple is famous for its annual Rath Yatra, or chariot festival, in which the three main deities are pulled on huge chariots. Unlike stone or metal deities in most Hindu temples, the idols of Jagannath, Balabhadra and Subhadra are made of wood and are ceremoniously replaced every 12 or 19 years.

The temple kitchen is the largest in the world, capable of feeding 100,000 people daily. The Mahaprasad offered here is considered highly sacred and is distributed without any discrimination of caste or creed.

The annual chariot festival attracts over 1 million devotees. The three chariots are newly built every year with specific dimensions: Nandighosa (45 feet high) for Jagannath, Taladhwaja (44 feet) for Balabhadra, and Darpadalana (43 feet) for Subhadra.

Top Sightseeing Places

Puri Beach

Golden sandy beach with views of sunrise, sea sports, and local seafood stalls.

Chilika Lake

Asia's largest brackish water lagoon, 50km from Puri, famous for dolphins and birds.

Konark Sun Temple

UNESCO World Heritage site, 35km from Puri, shaped like a giant chariot.

Narendra Tank

Sacred water tank near temple for ritual bathing, surrounded by small temples.

Grand Road

Main street for Rath Yatra procession, lined with shops selling handicrafts.

Gundicha Temple

Destination of Rath Yatra, where deities stay for 9 days during festival.

Odisha State Museum

Archaeological artifacts, palm leaf manuscripts, and traditional art.

Botanical Garden

Peaceful garden with rare plants, walking trails, and picnic spots.
