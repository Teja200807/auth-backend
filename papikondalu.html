Godavari's Scenic Gorge

Papi Kondalu, meaning "Papi Mountains," are steep hills lining the Godavari River in Andhra Pradesh, forming one of India's most scenic river gorges. The hills rise dramatically from the river, creating a breathtaking landscape that has been inhabited by tribal communities for centuries.

ðŸš¤ River Safari Paradise

The 80km boat safari from Rajahmundry to Papi Kondalu offers stunning views of the hills, waterfalls, and tribal villages. The journey passes through narrow gorges where the hills seem to touch the river surface.

The region is home to indigenous tribes like the Konda Dora, Konda Kammara, and Konda Kapu, who maintain traditional lifestyles and crafts. Their unique bamboo houses, terraced farming, and festivals offer cultural immersion opportunities for visitors.

Papi Kondalu forms part of the Papikonda Wildlife Sanctuary, protecting rich biodiversity including tigers, leopards, gaur, and numerous bird species. The area's ecological significance is enhanced by its location in the Eastern Ghats biodiversity hotspot.

Papi Kondalu is part of the Papikonda Wildlife Sanctuary, home to endangered species like the Indian python, king cobra, and numerous endemic plants. The river supports diverse aquatic life including the endangered Godavari mahseer fish.
