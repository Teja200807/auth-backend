About Araku Valley

Araku Valley is a picturesque hill station in the Eastern Ghats, known for its coffee plantations, waterfalls, and rich tribal culture. Located 114 km from Visakhapatnam, the valley sits at an altitude of 910-1,400 meters (3,000-4,600 ft) above sea level.

â˜• Coffee Capital

Araku Valley produces some of India's finest organic coffee. The Coffee House of Araku Valley has won international awards, and the Araku Origin coffee is exported worldwide.

The valley is home to 36 different tribal communities, primarily the Porja, Khond, and Valmiki tribes, who maintain their traditional lifestyle and crafts. The tribal museum showcases their rich cultural heritage.

Araku is famous for its scenic train journey from Visakhapatnam, passing through 58 tunnels and over 84 bridges, offering breathtaking views of the valley. The journey is considered one of India's most beautiful train rides.

The valley practices organic farming with coffee, pepper, oranges, and jackfruit. The Araku Agricultural Cooperative supports tribal farmers with sustainable practices.

Top Attractions

Araku Railway

Scenic train ride through 58 tunnels, 84 bridges with valley views.

Tribal Museum

Showcases culture, artifacts, and lifestyle of 36 local tribes.

Coffee Plantations

Organic coffee estates with guided tours and tasting sessions.

Katiki Waterfalls

50ft waterfall near Borra Caves, surrounded by dense forests.

Borra Caves

150-million-year-old limestone caves with stunning formations.

Padmapuram Gardens

Botanical gardens with tree huts, rose garden, and toy train.

Galikonda Viewpoint

Highest point in valley (4,500 ft) with panoramic views.

Tribal Dance Performances

Cultural shows featuring Dhimsa and other tribal dances.
